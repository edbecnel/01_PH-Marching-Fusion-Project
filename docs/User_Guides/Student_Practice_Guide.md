# Student Practice Guide

> **Status:** Maintained
> **Owner:** Philippine Marching Percussion Fusion Project
> **Applies To:** Drum & Lyre and marching percussion students
> **Last Reviewed:** 2026-07-16
> **Review Frequency:** Quarterly
> **Authoritative:** Yes

## Purpose

This guide orients **students** to the Philippine Marching Percussion Fusion practice packet. It explains how to use the technique chapters in this PDF, how your instructor may assign reading, and what to focus on in rehearsal.

Teaching methodology and pilot logistics live in the [Educational Handbook](Educational_Handbook.md) and [Instructor Introduction](Instructor_Introduction.md)—for instructors, not required student reading.

## How to Use This PDF

1. Read this guide once at the start of the term or pilot.
2. Follow your instructor's assignments using the **canonical names** in the table below—the same section titles appear in this PDF, the [Reference](../Reference/README.md) volume, and instructor materials.
3. Practice the embedded technique chapters in the order your section leader assigns (see [Suggested Learning Path](#suggested-learning-path)).
4. Use MuseScore files and practice audio when your instructor points you to repository scores and audio (not included in this PDF).

## What We Are Learning

The project builds a **distinctly Filipino** modern marching percussion style:

> **Modernization without imitation.**

That means we strengthen Philippine groove, festival energy, and ensemble communication—not copy foreign drum corps note-for-note.

As a student, focus on:

- **Groove before speed** — feel the rhythm cleanly before playing fast
- **Stick heights and clarity** — accents and taps must sound different
- **Listening** — match your section and respond in call-and-response passages
- **Movement with music** — energy and audience connection are part of the style
- **Ear first, notation second** — written exercises support what you hear in rehearsal

Your instructor applies the full [Identity Framework](../Specifications/Philippine_Marching_Percussion_Identity_Framework.md) when choosing material. You do not need to memorize policy documents—follow section leadership and the technique chapters in this packet.

## Suggested Learning Path

Your instructor may reorder these steps. Typical progression:

1. [Legato Stroke](../Reference/Legato_Stroke.md) — rebound, even heights, relaxed wrist
2. [Accent-Taps](../Reference/Accent_Taps.md) — contrast between accents and taps
3. [8-8-16 Exercise](../Reference/Eight_Eight_Sixteen.md) — endurance and hand-to-hand control
4. [Grid Exercises](../Reference/Grid_Exercises.md) — accent patterns and syncopation

Warm-ups in this project combine marching-percussion fundamentals with Philippine rhythmic vocabulary. See the [Hybrid Warm-Up System](<Educational_Handbook.md#Hybrid Warm-Up System>) for how instructors structure daily rehearsal.

## Practice Expectations

- **Come prepared** — sticks, practice pad or drum, water, and any assigned charts
- **Repeat correctly, then faster** — accuracy and sound quality before tempo
- **Mark your music** — pencil marks for sticking, accents, and reminders your instructor gives
- **Record only with permission** — ask before recording instructors or fellow students
- **Ask questions** — if a passage does not feel like Philippine groove, say so in section
