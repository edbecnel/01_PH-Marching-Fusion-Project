# Kaamulan Festival

> **Status:** Draft
> **Owner:** Philippine Marching Percussion Fusion Project
> **Applies To:** Cultural research and arranging inspiration
> **Last Reviewed:** 2026-07-13
> **Review Frequency:** Annual
> **Authoritative:** No

## Purpose

The Kaamulan Festival is one of the project's primary cultural reference points. This document records research topics and findings that inform the [Identity Framework](../Specifications/Philippine_Marching_Percussion_Identity_Framework.md).

Kaamulan is treated as **inspiration**, not as material to copy directly.

## Research Topics

- rhythmic layering
- movement and dance-percussion relationships
- audience interaction and community participation
- ceremonial pacing
- visual-musical integration
- regional diversity within festival performance

## Project Application

Insights from Kaamulan research should inform:

- festival energy in performance and arranging
- layered rhythmic writing in battery parts
- movement integrated with musical phrasing
- audience engagement concepts
- the [Philippine Percussion Sound Model](../Reference/Philippine_Percussion_Sound_Model.md)

## Findings

Research findings will be recorded here as field work and source analysis progress. See expanded profiles in [Indigenous Philippine Percussion](Indigenous_Philippine_Percussion.md).

Pilot instructors—including MONHS (Misamis Oriental National High School) collaborators from Bukidnon—are invited to contribute cultural guidance. See the [MONHS 2026 Pilot Brief](../User_Guides/MONHS_2026_Pilot_Brief.md) and [Instructor Introduction](../User_Guides/Instructor_Introduction.md).
