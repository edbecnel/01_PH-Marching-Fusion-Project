# Project Charter

## Project Name

The Philippine Marching Percussion Fusion Project

## Purpose

Establish a new, distinctly Filipino modern marching percussion tradition that preserves Philippine cultural identity while embracing modern musicianship—**modernization without imitation**.

See the [Philippine Marching Percussion Identity Framework](docs/Specifications/Philippine_Marching_Percussion_Identity_Framework.md) for the authoritative identity statement.

## Problem Being Addressed

Many Philippine Drum & Lyre and festival percussion groups rely primarily on rote learning. This preserves strong oral tradition and local feel, but can limit repeatability, rhythmic complexity, written preservation, curriculum development, and scalable instruction.

The project introduces notation and structured pedagogy without replacing the ear-based and culturally grounded practices that give Philippine percussion its character.

## Objectives

- Preserve authentic Philippine rhythmic identity.
- Establish a blended ear-and-notation teaching method.
- Create progressive warm-ups and educational materials.
- Develop original cadences and show arrangements.
- Document arranging, notation, rehearsal, and visual-design methods.
- Produce practical scores and audio tracks for performers.
- Test and refine the system with local musicians.

## Scope

### In Scope

- Marching percussion pedagogy
- Drumline and festival-banda arranging
- MuseScore Studio 4 notation
- Muse Drumline playback validation
- Warm-ups, cadences, and show arrangements
- Practice audio
- Visual-performance concepts
- Philippine cultural and rhythmic research

### Out of Scope for the Initial Stage

- Building custom music software
- Formal national curriculum accreditation
- Large-scale publishing or commercial distribution
- Full competitive field-show design
- Replacing traditional oral teaching methods

## Primary Stakeholders

- Project owner and arranger
- Local Philippine Drum & Lyre performers
- Music educators and section leaders — see [Instructor Introduction](docs/User_Guides/Instructor_Introduction.md)
- MONHS (Misamis Oriental National High School) Drum & Lyre Corps instructors (2026 pilot) — see [MONHS 2026 Pilot Brief](docs/User_Guides/MONHS_2026_Pilot_Brief.md)
- Schools and community festival organizations
- Future arrangers and contributors

## Success Criteria

The project succeeds when it produces material that is:

- recognizably Filipino;
- technically progressive;
- teachable to local performers;
- clearly notated;
- musically exciting;
- reusable by educators;
- supported by coherent documentation.

## Constraints

- Available local instruments may differ from standard DCI batteries.
- Reading ability may vary widely.
- Arrangements must remain practical for street and school environments.
- Cultural authenticity must take priority over imitation.
- Technical complexity must be introduced progressively.
