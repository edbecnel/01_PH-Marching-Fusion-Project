# BLUEPRINT PLAN: THE PHILIPPINE MARCHING PERCUSSION FUSION

## 1. PROJECT OVERVIEW

### Vision

The Philippine Marching Percussion Fusion Project is an effort to modernize traditional Philippine Drum & Lyre and festival street drumming by combining the best elements of three musical traditions into a uniquely Filipino performance style.

Rather than copying American drumlines, the objective is to create an authentic Philippine identity that preserves the powerful energy and cultural character of local street drumming while incorporating the technical sophistication of modern marching percussion.

The project blends three complementary influences:

- **Traditional Philippine Festival Drumming**
    
    - Raw energy
        
    - Powerful bass drum pulse
        
    - Native syncopations
        
    - Audience participation
        
    - Cultural identity
        
- **Drum Corps International (DCI)**
    
    - Rudimental precision
        
    - Advanced battery writing
        
    - Modern tenor movement
        
    - Dynamic phrasing
        
    - Professional notation
        
- **Historically Black Colleges & Universities (HBCU)**
    
    - Explosive showmanship
        
    - Swagger ("yabang")
        
    - Hip-hop influenced grooves
        
    - Crowd engagement
        
    - Visual entertainment
        

The ultimate goal is to establish a distinctly Filipino marching percussion style that can stand alongside international marching traditions while remaining unmistakably Philippine.

# 4. REVOLUTIONIZING PEDAGOGY: FROM ROTE TO NOTATION

Traditional Philippine drumlines are almost entirely taught by ear.

While this develops listening ability, it often limits rhythmic complexity, consistency, and long-term preservation of arrangements.

This project introduces formal written notation as a tool for expanding musical possibilities without sacrificing the oral traditions that give Philippine drumming its character.

## Software Foundation

All compositions will be written using MuseScore Studio 4's dedicated **Percussion – Marching** template.

## Hybrid Warm-Up System

Technical exercises will combine elite marching percussion fundamentals with familiar Philippine rhythmic vocabulary.

Examples include:

- Legatos
    
- Double-Strokes
    
- Accent-Taps
    
- 8-8-16
    
- Flam studies
    
- Grid exercises
    

The exercises will use DCI stick-height control while grouping accents around traditional festival syncopations such as Sinulog, Dinagyang, and other regional rhythmic patterns.

Players therefore learn advanced technique through grooves that already feel natural.

# 5. ARRANGING METHODOLOGY: THE THREE-LAYER FUSION

## Layer A — The Soul

Maintain the unmistakable pulse of Philippine festival drumming.

### Bass Drums

- Driving heartbeat
    
- Native syncopations
    
- Dance-oriented momentum
    

### Cymbals

- Lock into the pulse
    
- Reinforce energy
    
- Increase audience excitement
    

---

## Layer B — The Precision

Apply modern drum corps vocabulary over the native groove.

### Snares

Use:

- Hertas
    
- Cheese Flams
    
- Paradiddle-Diddles
    
- Tap Drags
    
- Grid Patterns
    
- Inverted Rudiments
    

while preserving the integrity of the underlying Philippine rhythm.

### Tenors

Introduce:

- Sweeps
    
- Scrapes
    
- Multi-tonal movement
    
- Split orchestrations
    

If only traditional single tenor drums are available, treat the section collectively as one melodic voice by distributing pitches among multiple players.

---

## Layer C — The Dynamic Contrast

Introduce dramatic contrast inspired by HBCU battery writing.

Examples include:

- Halftime grooves
    
- Hip-hop influenced breakdowns
    
- Metric modulation
    
- Tuplet features
    
- Explosive returns to full festival tempo
    

The objective is to surprise audiences without losing the recognizable local pulse.

---

# 6. VISUAL IDENTITY: PHILIPPINE "YABANG"

The visual program should be unmistakably Filipino rather than simply imitating American marching styles.

Traditional influences may include:

- Tinikling-inspired weight shifts
    
- Indigenous warrior stances
    
- Regional folk dance movements
    
- Filipino festival performance traditions
    

Modern influences include:

- Back-sticking
    
- Side flashes
    
- Overhead stick visuals
    
- HBCU-style swagger
    
- Crowd interaction
    

The result should communicate confidence, pride, and uniquely Philippine stage presence.

# 8. GUIDING DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Every musical decision should satisfy four core principles:

1. Preserve the soul of Philippine street drumming.
    
2. Introduce modern technical vocabulary without overwhelming traditional players.
    
3. Create performances that are visually exciting as well as musically sophisticated.
    
4. Build a sustainable educational framework that future Philippine drumlines can adopt, expand, and ultimately make their own.
    

This project is not intended to replace Philippine drumming traditions. Its purpose is to elevate them through modern pedagogy, world-class arranging techniques, and a uniquely Filipino artistic identity.
